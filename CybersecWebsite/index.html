About Us

The Cybersecurity Innovation Center (CIC) at West Virginia State University (WVSU) is a key pillar in advancing critical infrastructure security. CIC provides students with hands-on experience in advanced labs, promotes research and innovation, and helps position WVSU as a leading HBCU in cybersecurity education. The center supports hundreds of students, fostering a strong culture of technology and innovation. CIC plays a vital role in protecting critical infrastructures, with a special focus on cybersecurity in agriculture and other essential sectors. Not only is CIC shaping the future of students, but it also holds a leading role in advancing the cybersecurity sector through research, collaboration, and real-world problem-solving.

Opportunities

Collaborative Programs

The Google Clinic Partnership represents our commitment to real-world experience and community service. Through this initiative, our students provide free cybersecurity evaluations to local small businesses, applying their classroom knowledge to protect our community's digital infrastructure.

Research Opportunities

Our Research Program offers students the chance to conduct cutting-edge research in cybersecurity, AI, and computer science. Selected projects receive funding and support, with opportunities for publication in academic journals, helping students establish their presence in the field.

Scholarships

The NobleReach Scholars Program offers recent graduates fully compensated, one-year positions in AI, computing & cybersecurity within government agencies. In 2025, 28 scholars were selected from over 1,000 applicants, working with prestigious organizations including the US Department of Defense and various city innovation authorities.

The Cyber Ready Professionals IT Audit Fundamentals Scholarship ($4,415 value) provides specialized training in IT auditing, cybersecurity, and risk management, preparing students for professional audit roles with comprehensive certification preparation.

The KCC Cybersecurity Scholarship ($1,500) supports students pursuing excellence in cybersecurity education.

The NSF CyberCorps Scholarship for Service (SFS) offers full tuition support for master's degrees in computer science or computer engineering, including generous stipends and annual professional development funds. Recipients attend exclusive job fairs in Washington, DC, connecting with premier employers like the National Security Agency and National Laboratories.
